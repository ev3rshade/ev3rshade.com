The air spills into my lungs as I breathe in. I hold it and close my eyes. 

When I listen to the silence my eyelids flutter through the darkness in colors I cannot taste, motions I cannot hear. The images that linger in my vision are effaced of sensation, blurred on the edges, and I am left to caress the fringes of each with my cold, unfeeling heart.

It twitches with the memory of being alive.

Even after all the laughs that have drawn lines across my face, my lips forget their gentle curve in the face of a mirror. My face curls upward in a trembling grimace in an effort to remember how to smile. Yet my heart beats with blood not with feeling and the facade of emotion is broken when I bear the sight of my miserable face. 

Without their lilting cadence, memories are merely photos to be sorted into boxes and transcripts to be clipped and analyzed. Yet with nothing else to cling to, my mind plays them again and again and again on a broken tape, playing it back, playing it forward, senselessly wrapping it around my heart to bind it shut. Arranged, rearranged, and scattered the indiscernible shadows shift through my mind until they are splintered apart as I open my eyes. I exhale, and the air spills out of my lungs.
