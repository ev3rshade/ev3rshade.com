underneath it all maybe i haven't changed

---
i
slipping in and out of consciousness i was reminded of reading and creating over and over in an endless cycle. they were the swaths of time i forget the world around me, where my every waking moment was spent in an existence that was separate from who i was. by captivating my imagination and selling my soul to create, i minimized my emotional attachment to reality.

---
ii
the inner narrative never ceases.

in truth, my head is never empty, it is always thinking about what it last saw, who it last spoke to, what it wants to create and do and feel in the spaces it fails to touch with its imagination alone.

it's easier to explain to people there's nothing going on in my brain, so i tell them another lie.

---
iii
i feel the most at ease in the spaces i can press my body into. a flat surface against my back and my legs scrunched up and feel pressed against another flat surface. 

---
iv
sometimes i visualize myself crying and screaming and tearing off all my hair. sometimes it's another violent action. i do not fulfill my imagination because people will find me disturbing.
