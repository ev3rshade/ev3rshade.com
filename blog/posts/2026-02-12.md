under my goals for 2026 i wrote "being more minimalistic".

around september of last year i stopped listening to music while studying and coding. i wanted to try something new to help me focus, and to my surprise the lack of noise did help my diminishing attention span bounce back. the lack of background noise made me realize that quiet spaces help me collect my thoughts, something that does need to be done if i want to understand the learning material better.

after about half a year of studying like this, i have grown really comfortable with the presence of silence in different spaces from a failing conversation between me and another person to the stillness that settles in the room when i am alone washing the dishes.

from this experience among others, i have begun to gravitate towards the principles of minimalism. to focus on the things that matter to me, i must eliminate the noise that clutters my space.
